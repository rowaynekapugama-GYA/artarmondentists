Problems to watch for

Signs to watch for in growing children

Malocclusions — or "bad bites" — like these can benefit from early diagnosis and a simple orthodontic evaluation. Spotting them while your child is still growing usually means shorter, gentler treatment.

Crossbite — front teethTop front teeth sit behind the bottom teeth.
Crossbite — back teethTop back teeth bite inside the bottom teeth.
CrowdingNot enough room, so teeth overlap or twist.
Open biteFront teeth don't meet when the back teeth close.
ProtrusionUpper front teeth stick out beyond the lowers.
Deep biteUpper front teeth cover too much of the lowers.
UnderbiteLower teeth sit in front of the upper teeth.
SpacingNoticeable gaps between the teeth.
Oral habitsThumb or finger-sucking affecting development.
Also worth a check if you notice…

early or late loss of baby teeth · difficulty chewing or biting · mouth breathing · jaws that shift or make sounds · speech difficulties · biting the cheek or roof of the mouth · facial imbalance · grinding or clenching.

A staged approach

The three stages of early orthodontic treatment

Because your child's teeth, jaws and muscles are still developing, treating at the right stage means more effective, longer-lasting results.

1

Prevention & interception

Ages 3–6 · Baby teeth

Guiding healthy jaw development, facial symmetry and habits before problems take hold.

  • Jaw position & symmetry
  • Tooth spacing & alignment
  • Breathing & oral habits — thumb-sucking, mouth breathing, tongue thrusting
2

Interception & correction

Ages 7–9 · Mixed dentition

As permanent teeth emerge, we check how the jaws align and whether there's room for all teeth — especially the canines.

  • Braces or removable appliances
  • Space maintainers to guide eruption
  • Palatal expanders for narrow arches & breathing
3

Correction & rehabilitation

Ages 10+ · Permanent teeth

Final alignment of the teeth and jaws for a healthy bite, comfortable jaw and a confident smile.

  • Traditional braces or clear aligners
  • Bite correction for healthy jaw function
  • Discreet, nearly-invisible options

Why start early?

Why early orthodontic treatment?

Orthodontics does far more than straighten teeth. Starting while your child is still growing helps:

Build a healthy bite

Aligns the jaws and chewing muscles so the bite works comfortably.

Improve the smile & profile

Supports balanced facial development and smile aesthetics.

Prevent bigger problems

Heads off long-term issues like crowding and misalignment.

Simplify future treatment

Often makes any later orthodontics shorter and easier.

What to expect in your child's journey

  • X-rays & digital scans — a detailed view of teeth, jawbones and facial structure to plan the most effective treatment.
  • Dental impressions & models — so we can plan precise, gentle tooth movement.
  • Before & after photos — so your child can see their amazing smile transformation.
